**Continual Learning**

As machines continue to advance and take on increasingly complex tasks, their ability to learn and adapt over time becomes increasingly crucial. This is where my area of interest and expertise, continual learning, comes into play. Also known as lifelong learning, continual learning refers to a machine's ability to acquire new skills and knowledge without forgetting what it previously learned. This poses a specific challenge in the field of deep learning, as neural networks often have a tendency to "forget" when presented with new information.

---

**Meta Learning**

Another research area that I’m interested in is meta-learning, which is also referred to as "learning to learn". Given a distribution of tasks, the idea is to train an agent in such a way that it captures the commonality among those tasks. This allows the agent to quickly adapt to new, unseen tasks (with similar structure) using only a few samples and minimal updates.

---


**Continual Learning $$\cap$$ Meta-Learning**

I've recently been exploring the intersections of continual and meta-learning. The main idea here is to train models that can autonomously develop mechanisms, enabling them to adapt faster and forget less when trained on a sequence of tasks. Essentially, the agent should learn the optimal configuration for a meta-objective, such as knowledge preservation over time, without being explicitly instructed on how to achieve it. I’m excited to see how this research direction can be leveraged to create intelligent systems that are more versatile and adaptable for real-world applications.
