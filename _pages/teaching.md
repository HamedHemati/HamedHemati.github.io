**Continual Learning**

As machines continue to advance and take on increasingly complex tasks, the ability for them to learn and adapt over time becomes increasingly crucial. This is where my area of interest and expertise, continual Learning, comes into play. Also known as lifelong learning, Continual Learning is the ability for a machine to acquire new skills and knowledge without forgetting what it had previously learned. It's specifically a challenging problem in the field of deep learning as neural networks often have a tendency to "forget" when presented with new information.

---

**Meta Learning**

Another research area that I’m interested in is meta-learning, which is also referred to as learning to learn. Given a distribution of tasks, the ideas is to train an agent in a way that it can capture the commonality among those tasks so that it can quickly adapt to new unseen tasks (with similar structure) using a small number of samples and updates. 

---


**Continual Learning $$\cap$$ Meta-Learning**

I've recently been exploring the intersections of continual and meta-learning, where the main idea is to train models that automatically learn a mechanism that can enable them to adapt faster and forget less when trained on a sequence of tasks. That means the agent should automatically learn the the best configuration to optimize a meta-objective, which can be knowledge preservation over time, without exactly telling it how to do it. I’m excited to see how this research direction can be applied to make intelligent systems that are more versatile and adaptable in real-world applications.
